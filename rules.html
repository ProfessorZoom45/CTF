Start Here — Learn CTF Fast

If you are brand new, do not try to memorize everything at once. Learn the game in this order: board → summoning → battle → End Phase → win conditions.

Quick loop: Draw 1 card, play cards in Action Phase, decide whether to attack in Battle Phase, then choose an End Phase action or END TURN.
Thing to learn firstWhat it means in plain language
ChiYour life total. You start at 10,000. If it hits 0, you lose immediately.
P and CPP = Pressure when a Catalyst attacks. CP = Counter Pressure when a Catalyst defends.
VoidThe graveyard. Destroyed, discarded, and sacrificed cards go here.
BoxYour capture pile. Enemy Catalysts you beat in CP usually go here instead of the Void.
RFGRemoved From Game for this duel. Extracted cards go here.
END TURNAlways legal. Costs nothing. It is the safe End Phase option when you do not want to spend a Catalyst.
Beginner warning: Most confusion comes from mixing up Kill, Capture, Destroy, and Extract. A battle can destroy to the Void, capture to the Box, or do no removal at all depending on P vs P or P vs CP.

Battle Cheat Sheet — Read This Before Your First Duel

When one Catalyst attacks another, use the attacker's P. Then look at the defender's position:

Defender positionWhat you comparePlain-language result
Pressure positionAttacker P vs defender PHigher P wins. The loser goes to the Void. The difference becomes Chi damage.
Counter Pressure positionAttacker P vs defender CPIf attacker wins, the defender usually goes to the Box. If attacker ties, nothing happens. If attacker loses, no Kill or Capture happens and the attacker controller takes Chi damage equal to the difference.

Four battle examples

ExampleMathOutcome
Your 2000 P attacks their 1500 P2000 > 1500Their Catalyst goes to the Void. You score +1 Kill. They take 500 Chi damage.
Your 2000 P attacks their 1700 CP2000 > 1700Their Catalyst goes to your Box. That is a Capture, not a Kill. No Chi damage.
Your 2000 P attacks their 2000 CP2000 = 2000Tie. Nothing is destroyed or captured. No Chi damage.
Your 1800 P attacks their 2200 CP1800 < 2200No Kill. No Capture. You take 400 Chi damage because the attacker lost to CP.
Great Card exception: if you beat a Great Card that is in Counter Pressure position, it does not go to the Box. It goes to the Void and counts as a Kill.

Combat — Clean Battle System Explanation

Every attack follows the same order:

  1. Choose your attacking Catalyst. It uses P.
  2. Check the defender's position.
  3. If the defender is in P, compare P vs P.
  4. If the defender is in CP, compare P vs CP.
  5. Apply the correct destination: Void, Box, or no movement.
  6. Then apply any Chi damage.
ScenarioResultChi Damage?Why it matters
Your P > Opponent POpponent Catalyst → VoidYES, equal to the differenceThis is a Kill.
Your P < Opponent PYour Catalyst → VoidYES, equal to the differenceYour opponent gets the Kill.
Your P = Opponent PBoth Catalysts → VoidNOMutual Kill. Each player gets +1 Kill.
Your P > Opponent CPOpponent Catalyst → your BoxNOThis is a Capture, not a Kill.
Your P > Opponent Great Card CPGreat Card → VoidNOGreat Cards never enter the Box, so this becomes a Kill.
Your P = Opponent CPNo destruction. No capture.NOTie. Battle ends with no movement.
Your P < Opponent CPNo destruction. No capture.YES, attacking controller takes the differenceAttacking into bigger CP punishes the attacker.
Direct attackNo defending CatalystYES, full attacking POnly legal if the opponent has no Catalysts in their Catalyst Zones.
Do not confuse these: Kill means a battle destroy that counts toward 7 Kills. Destroy by card effect is still removal, but it does not count as a Kill. Capture sends the defeated defender to the Box. Extract happens later in the End Phase from the Box.

Win Conditions — 3 Paths

All three are active simultaneously. You do NOT choose one at the start. The moment ANY one is achieved, that player wins IMMEDIATELY. Paths do NOT mix: 3 Kills + 4 Extractions ≠ a win.

Simultaneous Win Priority: Chi KO > 7 Kills > 7 Extractions.

Win Condition 1: Chi KO

Reduce opponent Chi from 10,000 to 0. Chi KO is instant — game ends the moment Chi hits 0. Logic damage from: ATK vs ATK battle (Pressure difference), direct attacks (full Pressure when all Catalyst Zones empty), and card effects as printed.

Win Condition 2: 7 Kills 🔒 LOCKED

Destroy 7 opponent Catalysts through battle only.

Win Condition 3: 7 Extractions

The most complex win path. Three stages:

Stage 1 — Capture: Your ATK Pressure must strictly exceed opponent's DEF Counter Pressure. Their Catalyst goes to YOUR Box. No Logic damage. Great Cards cannot be Captured.

Stage 2 — Box Management: Cards in your Box are resources. Opponent can Rescue their own cards from your Box (costs them 1 Catalyst + their End Phase).

Stage 3 — Extraction: End Phase: sacrifice 1 eligible Catalyst → Void. Move 1 opponent Catalyst from your Box to the RFG Zone permanently. +1 toward 7 Extractions.

The Box & Capture System

When your ATK Pressure strictly exceeds an opponent's DEF Counter Pressure, the defender is Captured and enters your Box. ATK = DEF = tie (bounce, nothing captured). Only Catalysts can enter the Box.

SituationOpponent's ChoiceResult
Your Catalyst in opp's BoxRescue (cost: 1 Catalyst → Void)Returns to YOUR Deck (shuffled in)
Your Catalyst in opp's BoxLeave itOpponent Extracts it → RFG permanently
No eligible CatalystsCannot RescueOpponent Extracts freely — winning position

End Phase — Mandatory Strategic Choice 🔒 LOCKED

An eligible Catalyst = a Catalyst in one of your Catalyst Zones that is not blocked by a tribute/sacrifice restriction. Great and non-Great Catalysts may be sacrificed. If you have at least one eligible Catalyst, you may choose any of these three paid actions:

ActionCostEffect
EXTRACTION1 eligible Catalyst → VoidMove 1 opponent Catalyst from YOUR Box to RFG permanently. +1 toward 7.
RESCUE1 eligible Catalyst → VoidReturn 1 of YOUR OWN Catalysts from opponent's Box to your Deck (shuffled).
DESTROY TRICK1 eligible Catalyst → VoidDestroy 1 Trick on the field — yours or opponent's, face-up or face-down.

After the End Phase action: discard to 7 cards if over. Extraction, Rescue, and Destroy Trick require an eligible Catalyst as the cost. Great and non-Great Catalysts may be sacrificed. END TURN is always valid and costs nothing.

Speed System 🔒 LOCKED

Palm Tricks = proactive (your turn, Link 1 only). Concealed Tricks = reactive (either turn, any chain position). Counter Tricks = highest priority (once on chain, only another Counter can respond above it). This split is strict — no exceptions.

KINDS System 🔒 LOCKED

A Catalyst may have multiple KINDS simultaneously — all count at once. If your card is [Warrior / Saiyan] and two buffs apply (one to Warriors, one to Saiyans), your card gets BOTH bonuses simultaneously.

21 KINDS: Warrior, Beast-Warrior, Beast, Machine, Fairy, Zombie, Dragon, Human, Ninja, Spellcaster, Fiend, Demon, Aqua, Psychic, Divine/Beast, Gunman, Saiyan, Gundam, Effect, Normal, Fusion.

Libra Summon 🔒 LOCKED

Place Normal Catalysts in the two outermost Libra Zones. Their Levels become your Scales. You may Libra Summon up to 5 Catalysts whose Levels are strictly between those Scales. The whole Libra Summon counts as exactly 1 Special Summon and causes exactly 1 Shotgun draw total.

Libra Zone Catalysts are treated as Trick cards for targeting. They cannot be used as End Phase sacrifices. If a Scale is destroyed mid-combo, already-summoned Catalysts remain on the field.

Great Cards

Any card with "Great" in its name is a Great Card — Catalysts, Palm Tricks, Concealed Tricks, any card type. Great Card Catalysts have special restrictions:

Max 1 copy per nameCannot run 2–3 copies of any Great Card.
Max 5 Great Catalysts/deckEven if you own more, only 5 total Great Card Catalysts.
NEVER enter the BoxCannot be Captured. ATK beats Great DEF → Void as +1 Kill.
Cannot be ExtractedExtraction attempt → Void as Kill.
Great Catalyst sacrificeGreat and non-Great eligible Catalysts may be sacrificed for paid End Phase actions.
CAN be Killed in ATK vs ATKNo combat immunity — destroyed normally in battle.
Great Trick cardsSame copy limit. Otherwise governed by their card type's normal rules.
