System Overview

What Makes CTF Different

🔥

3 Simultaneous Win Paths

Drain opponent Chi from 10,000 to 0 (Chi KO), destroy 7 Catalysts through battle (7 Kills), or Capture into your Box then Extract 7 permanently (7 Extractions). All three paths are live every turn — switch mid-match when your opponent over-commits to one defense.

⚔️

Proactive vs Reactive Split

Palm Tricks are always proactive — your turn only, always Link 1. Concealed Tricks are always reactive — set face-down, activate on opponent's turn. Counter Tricks lock the chain — only another Counter can respond above them. No timing ambiguity.

📦

The Box & Capture System

When your Pressure beats an opponent's Counter Pressure, their Catalyst enters your Box — not the Void. Cards in the Box fuel the Extraction win path. The Box vs Void distinction is the strategic heart of CTF.

⚖️

The Libra System

Place two Normal Catalysts in the outermost Libra Zones. Their Levels define a range — summon any Catalyst whose Level falls strictly between the two scales. The entire Libra Summon counts as 1 Special Summon.

🎯

End Phase — Mandatory Choice

Every End Phase with an eligible Catalyst forces a choice: End Turn, Extraction, Rescue, or Destroy Trick. Three of the four cost a Catalyst sacrifice. This tension between offense, recovery, and board control defines every turn.

🛡️

Shotgun Rule

Every Special Summon gives your opponent a free draw — 1st through 5th, no exceptions. Hard cap of 5 Special Summons per turn. This anti-combo safety valve keeps games balanced and punishes overextension.

Victory Paths

3 Ways to Win

I

Chi KO

Reduce opponent Chi from 10,000 to 0 through Logic damage. ATK vs ATK battle deals the Pressure difference. Direct attacks (when all opponent Catalyst Zones are empty) deal full Pressure. Chi KO is instant — game ends the moment Chi hits 0.

II

7 Kills

Destroy 7 opponent Catalysts through battle only. ATK vs ATK wins, Mutual Kills, and ATK beating a Great Card in DEF all count. Card effects and Destroy Trick send cards to Void as destroyed/discarded — those are NOT Kills.

III

7 Extractions

Capture opponent DEF Catalysts into your Box (ATK beats Counter Pressure), then sacrifice your own Catalysts during End Phase to Extract them permanently to the RFG Zone. The slowest but most strategically layered path.

Card System

Core Card Types

Catalysts

Primary units with Pressure (ATK) & Counter Pressure (DEF). 8 Alignments, stackable KINDS. Normal Catalysts (Lore text, no effect) are required for Libra Zones.

Palm Tricks

Proactive spells — your Action Phase only. Can NEVER be counter-type. Always Link 1 on any chain. Type: Palm. 🔒

Concealed Tricks

Reactive traps — set face-down, activate on either turn. Cannot activate same turn set. Only card playable on opponent's turn. Type: Concealed. 🔒

Field Tricks

Environment cards in Main Deck. 1 per player active. Effects refresh at Ignition Phase. New one replaces old. Type: Field. 🔒

Fusion Cards

Fusion Deck only (0–15). Requires named materials to Void + Fusion enabler. Entire Fusion Summon = 1 Special Summon. Shotgun Rule applies.

Great Cards

Any card with "Great" in its name. Great Catalysts: 1 copy each, max 5/deck. Can NEVER enter the Box. Cannot be Tributed for End Phase. CAN be Killed in ATK vs ATK battle.

New Player

Quick Start

Build Your Deck

40–60 cards in your Main Deck — Catalysts, Palm Tricks, Concealed Tricks, and Field Tricks shuffled together. Add up to 15 Fusion Cards in a separate Fusion Deck. Side Deck: 0–15 cards for Best of 3.

Draw & Deploy

Start with 10,000 Chi and 5 cards in hand. Draw 1 each turn. Normal Summon 1 Catalyst per turn — Level 1–4 free, Level 5–6 costs 1 Tribute, Level 7+ costs 2. Deck out is NOT a loss — skip draw and continue.

Play Through 7 Phases

Turn Start → Draw → Ignition → Action → Battle → Resolution → End. The Action Phase is your main play window. Battle Phase is always optional. The End Phase forces a mandatory strategic choice.

Carry The Flame

Adapt every turn. Chase Chi KO, 7 Kills, or 7 Extractions — or threaten multiple paths. The player who forces two simultaneous threats wins. Play online at perfecttiminggaming.com.
